About Me

I'm an enthusiastic Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) student who loves building smart and useful tech projects. So far, I’ve worked on 3 practical projects that gave me great hands-on experience. One of them is a Water Level Indicator using an ultrasonic sensor and ESP32, which helps monitor and control water levels automatically — it can alert users when the tank is about to overflow or go empty. I also created an RFID-based Attendance System to make student attendance faster and more accurate, cutting down manual effort by over 70%. Another project I worked on is a Smart Parking System, which detects available parking slots in real-time, helping reduce the time drivers spend looking for a spot.

These projects have helped me understand how to turn classroom learning into real-world solutions, and I’m excited to keep building smart and helpful electronic systems in the future.
